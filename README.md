# The Deciding Mile

**Mental Skills for the Moment Every Endurance Race Is Won or Lost**

A first-principles sport psychology framework for endurance athletes — delivered as a course, an app, and an audiobook.

## What This Is

The Deciding Mile consolidates perspectives from 4+ sport psychology authors, 15+ hours of academic content, and 6 years of field-tested coaching into a single coherent framework. It starts from clearly explained first principles, provides actionable tools at every step, and is designed to be consumed — not just purchased.

## The Problem

Athletes spend thousands of hours building fitness but zero hours building the mental toolkit to actually use it. When racing gets chaotic — and it always does — they collapse not because their legs fail, but because their mind does. Existing sport psych content is either:

- **Too academic** — dense textbooks that never leave the shelf
- **Too motivational** — "just believe in yourself" with no mechanism
- **Too narrow** — injury-only, or running-only, or visualization-only
- **Too passive** — courses that are consumed but never applied

## The Solution

Five progressive modules. Each built on one first principle. Each ending with a tool you practice — not just learn. Delivered in a format that creates behavior change, not just knowledge.

## Differentiators

1. **First principles, not topic salad** — each module starts with one idea in one sentence
2. **Coaching voice** — "Racing is chaos. Mike Tyson said everyone has a plan until they get punched in the mouth. EXACTLY."
3. **Every lesson ends with something you DO** — named, repeatable protocols
4. **Sport-agnostic within endurance** — gravel, road, XC ski, running, triathlon
5. **Progressive stack** — Module 3 can't work without Module 2. This creates narrative arc.
6. **Triple-format delivery** — course (learn), app (practice daily), audiobook (absorb passively)

## Framework: 6 Modules

1. **The Two Brains** — Your brain has two systems. Performance lives in the gap between them.
2. **The Stories You Tell Yourself** — You will never outperform your self-concept.
3. **Seeing Before Doing** — The brain doesn't fully distinguish imagined from experienced.
4. **The Zone and The Red Line** — Peak performance isn't trying harder. It's matching challenge to skill.
5. **Race Day** — Every mental tool, assembled into a concrete competition protocol.
6. **When It All Goes Wrong** — Adversity is the norm. Resilience is a skill, not a trait.
