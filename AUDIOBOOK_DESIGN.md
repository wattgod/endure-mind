# The Deciding Mile — Audiobook Design

## Format

**Narrative nonfiction + guided practice hybrid**

Not a textbook read aloud. Not a motivational speech. A *story* that teaches — weaving first principles, athlete anecdotes, coaching voice, and science into something people actually want to listen to on a 3-hour ride.

## Voice

The Stoic Coach — direct, warm, no-bullshit. Sounds like a coach talking to you at a coffee shop after a race, not a professor at a lectern.

Key tonal anchors:
- "Racing is chaos. Mike Tyson said everyone has a plan until they get punched in the mouth. EXACTLY."
- "God it sounds dumb, doesn't it? Too bad — do it anyway."
- "Your mind, just like your heart and muscles, is trainable."

## Structure

~6 hours total. 6 chapters matching the 6 course modules. Each chapter: 45-60 minutes.

### Chapter Flow (Each Chapter)

```
1. HOOK (2-3 min)
   A specific race/training moment that embodies the chapter's principle.
   Vivid, sensory, first-person. The listener should feel the gravel under their tires.

2. THE PRINCIPLE (5-8 min)
   The first principle stated simply, then unpacked with the science behind it.
   No jargon. Analogies. "Your brain is like a governor on an engine..."

3. THE SCIENCE (8-12 min)
   The research, explained conversationally. Name the researchers.
   "A guy named Tim Noakes in South Africa figured out something wild..."
   Include enough to be credible, not enough to be a lecture.

4. THE TOOLS (10-15 min)
   The practical techniques from the course, narrated as instructions.
   "Here's what you're going to do. Next time you're on the bike and your
   heart rate spikes before an interval, do the 6-2-7..."

5. ATHLETE STORIES (5-10 min)
   Real examples. Your coaching clients (anonymized). Your own experiences.
   Gravel Grit war stories. The attack on the climb. The god dammit moment.

6. THE CLOSE (2-3 min)
   Restate the principle. Connect it to the next chapter.
   Leave the listener wanting to apply it immediately.
```

### Chapter Breakdown

| Chapter | Title | Core Narrative |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Two Brains | Open with a race-start panic attack story. Introduce the reactive/deliberate brain split. The 6-2-7 as the bridge. |
| 2 | The Stories You Tell Yourself | Open with an athlete who had the legs but not the belief. Identity, self-efficacy, the schema statement. |
| 3 | Seeing Before Doing | Open with the "god dammit" attack memory. Visualization science. The highlight reel. If-then disaster plans. |
| 4 | The Zone and The Red Line | Open with a flow-state ride description — the day everything clicked. Then contrast with the day it didn't. What's different? |
| 5 | Race Day | Open with a race-morning sequence — alarm, nerves, warm-up, gun. Walk through the entire protocol as a narrative. |
| 6 | When It All Goes Wrong | Open with a DNF or injury story. The emotional rollercoaster. Coping. The long road back. End with: "Mental game is a habit, not a goal." |

### Bonus: Guided Practice Tracks

Included as a separate section at the end of the audiobook (or available as bonus content):
- 6-2-7 Breathing (5 min)
- Highlight Reel Construction (10 min)
- Pre-Race Protocol (15 min)
- The Grit Stack Walk-Through (5 min)

## Production Notes

- **Narration**: Matt Rowe (author narration preferred for coaching voice authenticity)
- **Distribution**: Audible, Apple Books, Google Play, direct download from landing page
- **Companion**: Reference app/course for exercises mentioned in the book
- **Length target**: 5.5-6.5 hours (sweet spot for Audible engagement)

## Why Audiobook Matters

Endurance athletes spend 5-15 hours/week training. Most of that time their ears are available. An audiobook is consumed during the activity itself — not competing for screen time. This is the only format that meets athletes where they already are: on the bike, on the run, on the trail.

The audiobook sells the framework. The app trains the skills. The course provides the depth. They are complementary, not redundant.
