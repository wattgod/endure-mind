# Grit Stack Walk-Through — Guided Audio Script

**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Lesson:** 5.4 — Deliver
**Voice:** Intense but controlled. This script simulates a race crisis. The voice should match — direct, urgent, but never panicked. The coach in the room when things are going sideways.
**Music:** None.

---

## Script

[2-second silence]

You're in the middle of a race. Something just went wrong. Maybe you got dropped. Maybe your stomach turned. Maybe the pace feels unsustainable and your brain is telling you to quit. Maybe you looked at your watch and the numbers are bad. Maybe your legs just stopped cooperating and you don't know why.

It doesn't matter what it is. What matters is what you do next.

This is the Grit Stack. Five steps. You've practiced this. Now run it.

[3-second pause]

**Step One: Breathe.**

One cycle of 6-2-7. Right now. Don't wait until you feel ready — you won't feel ready. That's the point.

In through the nose... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out through the mouth... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

One cycle. That's all it takes to interrupt the panic cascade. Your heart rate just ticked down. Your shoulders just dropped a fraction. The chimp is still screaming — but you just bought yourself a few seconds of clarity.

[2-second pause]

**Step Two: Detach.**

Name what's happening. Say it — out loud if you can, internally if you can't.

"That's the chimp."

The pain, the panic, the urge to stop, the catastrophic predictions about the next hour — that's the chimp. It's not you. It's not reality. It's your limbic system doing what it's designed to do — sounding the alarm. You heard the alarm. You don't have to obey it.

[5-second pause]

**Step Three: Reframe.**

Deploy your Performance Statement. The one you memorized. The one you wrote for exactly this moment.

Say it now. Whatever it is — technical, pain response, or process. Say it with conviction.

[5-second pause]

Say it again.

[5-second pause]

That statement is your answer to the chimp. The chimp said "stop." You said something better. The chimp talks first. You talk last.

[3-second pause]

**Step Four: Execute.**

Commit to the next segment. Not the finish. Not the rest of the race. The next checkpoint. The next aid station. The next mile marker. The next minute.

What's your next target? Name it. How far away is it? How long will it take?

That's your world right now. Nothing before it. Nothing after it. Just that.

[5-second pause]

Now go. Execute. Full commitment to that single target. When you reach it, you'll run the stack again if you need to. But right now — just this segment.

[5-second pause]

**Step Five: Segment.**

You made it. You're at the checkpoint. Now — and only now — pick the next one. Reset. If the chimp is back, start from Step One. Breathe. Detach. Reframe. Execute. Segment. Again.

The Grit Stack is a loop. You don't run it once. You run it as many times as the race demands. Each time you complete a cycle, you've proven something to yourself. You've proven that the chimp said stop — and you didn't.

[5-second pause]

That's the Grit Stack. Breathe. Detach. Reframe. Execute. Segment. Five steps. The moment every tool in this course was built for.

Now go practice it — in training, not just in your head. The next time a hard interval makes you want to quit, run the stack. Make it automatic. So when the race demands it, you don't think. You execute.

[3-second silence]

[End]
