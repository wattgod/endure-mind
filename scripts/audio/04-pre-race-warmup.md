# Pre-Race Mental Warm-Up — Guided Audio Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Lesson:** 5.2 — Race Morning
**Voice:** The Stoic Coach at his most composed. This is the last thing the athlete hears before racing. Calm authority. Zero fluff.
**Music:** None. This needs to work in a parking lot, a hotel room, or a start corral with noise around.

---

## Script

[3-second silence]

It's race day. The physical preparation is done — you did the training, you did the taper, you packed the bag. None of that changes in the next 15 minutes. What changes in the next 15 minutes is the state of your mind when the gun goes off.

This is your Pre-Race Mental Warm-Up. Four phases. Fifteen minutes. You know every tool we're about to use — you've practiced them. Now we deploy them in sequence.

Find a spot where you won't be interrupted. Standing or sitting. Eyes closed or soft-focused — whatever feels right.

[3-second pause]

**Phase One: Breathing. Minutes one through five.**

We're starting with the 6-2-7. You know the rhythm. Five minutes of it. The goal is to take your nervous system from wherever it is right now — probably running hot — and bring it to baseline. Not flat. Not sleepy. Baseline. Ready to be calibrated.

Here we go. First cycle.

In through the nose... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out through the mouth... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Again.

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Keep going. I'll count two more, then you'll continue on your own for the remaining time.

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Continue on your own. Same rhythm. I'll be quiet for about two minutes.

[120-second silence]

[2-second pause]

Good. Let your breathing return to normal. Notice where you are now compared to where you were five minutes ago. The dial should have moved.

[3-second pause]

**Phase Two: Highlight Reel. Minutes six through eight.**

Three minutes. Three scenes. You know the reel — you've built it, you've rehearsed it. First person. Maximum detail. Run it now.

Scene one: your empowering memory. The moment that proves what you're capable of. Go there.

[30-second pause]

Feel the power. Feel the version of you that showed up that day.

[15-second pause]

Scene two: the crucial moment in today's race. The hardest part. Visualize yourself executing — not winning, executing. Form solid. Breathing controlled. Self-talk on script.

[30-second pause]

You're in control. The chimp is talking. You're not listening.

[15-second pause]

Scene three: the full arc. Start to finish. See yourself performing today — prepared, present, executing the plan.

[30-second pause]

Let the images settle. That's your preview. Your brain just rehearsed today's performance. Now let's tell it who's showing up.

[3-second pause]

**Phase Three: Schema Statement. Minutes nine and ten.**

Say your Athletic Schema Statement. Out loud if you can. If not, say it in your head with conviction. The strength and the aspiration. Who you are and what you want.

[5-second pause]

Say it now.

[10-second pause]

Say it again. Slower. Mean it.

[10-second pause]

One more time. This is who shows up today. Not the anxious version. Not the doubting version. This version.

[10-second pause]

Now let it settle. Don't analyze it. Just let the statement sit in your chest.

[10-second pause]

**Phase Four: Activation Calibration. Minutes eleven through fifteen.**

This is where you customize. You know your optimal arousal zone from Lesson 1.3. You know whether you tend to run too hot or too cold before races. The next five minutes are about moving the dial to YOUR zone.

[3-second pause]

Check in with yourself right now. On the 1-to-10 arousal scale — where are you? Don't overthink it. What's the number?

[5-second pause]

If you're above your zone — if the number is too high, if your heart rate is elevated, if your hands are shaking, if you feel jittery — stay with the breathing. Slow 6-2-7 cycles. Drop your shoulders. Unclench your jaw. Let the parasympathetic system do its work.

[30-second pause]

If you're below your zone — if you feel flat, if there's no urgency, if you feel like you're going to brunch instead of a race — it's time to fire up. Deploy your motivational self-talk. Your Performance Statements. The ones that wake something up. Say them now — with force. Stand up if you're sitting. Move your body. Shake your hands out. Activate.

[30-second pause]

Wherever you are, spend the next two minutes moving toward your number. You know the tools. Breathing to come down. Self-talk and movement to come up. Find your zone.

[120-second silence]

[3-second pause]

Check in one more time. Where's the dial now?

[5-second pause]

That's your number. That's your state. You've spent 15 minutes building it with precision — breathing to reset, visualization to rehearse, schema to anchor identity, calibration to dial in arousal.

This is the version of you that races today. Not the one who was lying in bed at midnight doing sleep math. This one. The prepared one.

Go race.

[3-second silence]

[End]
