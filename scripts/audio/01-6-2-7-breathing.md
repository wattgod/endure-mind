# 6-2-7 Breathing Protocol — Guided Audio Script

**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Lesson:** 1.4 — The 6-2-7
**Voice:** Calm, measured, unhurried. Not spa-music soft — coach-steady. Think post-race debrief tone, not meditation app.
**Music:** None. Silence between instructions. The breathing IS the soundtrack.

---

## Script

[3-second silence]

Find a position where you can breathe deeply. Sitting or standing — doesn't matter. If you're in a car, stay in the car. If you're on the couch, stay on the couch. Just make sure your chest is open and your shoulders aren't up by your ears. Drop them. Good.

We're going to do the 6-2-7. You already know the formula. Inhale through your nose for six seconds. Hold for two. Exhale through your mouth for seven. The exhale is longer than the inhale — that's the whole point. That extended exhale triggers your parasympathetic nervous system. It tells your body the threat is over. And the counting keeps your chimp busy while the breathing does the work.

Don't overthink the counting. If you lose track, pick it back up. This isn't a test.

[2-second pause]

First cycle. Here we go.

Breathe in through your nose... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Exhale slowly through your mouth... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Good. Again.

In through the nose... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out through the mouth... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Notice your shoulders. If they crept back up, drop them again. Third cycle.

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

You should be feeling it now. Heart rate settling. The noise in your head getting a little quieter. That's not magic — that's your vagus nerve doing exactly what it's designed to do. Keep going.

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[2-second pause]

Last cycle on my count. Then you'll do three more on your own.

In... two... three... four... five... six.

Hold... two.

Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[3-second pause]

Now three more cycles on your own. Same rhythm. I'll be quiet.

[60-second silence — learner does 3 cycles independently]

[2-second pause]

That's it. Seven cycles. You just practiced the single fastest tool for resetting your nervous system. The goal is to get so familiar with this rhythm that you don't need me counting for you. That it becomes automatic. Something you can deploy on a start line, in the middle of a race, lying in bed the night before — without thinking about it.

Five minutes a day for one week. Then it's yours.

[3-second silence]

[End]
