# Progressive Muscle Relaxation — Guided Audio Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Lesson:** 1.3 (supplementary — linked from Know Your Zone for athletes who tend over-aroused)
**Voice:** Slower than the 6-2-7 script. Steady, low, measured. Pace between instructions matters — give muscles time to release.
**Music:** None. Silence is the point.

---

## Script

[3-second silence]

Lie down if you can. If you can't, sit somewhere comfortable with your feet flat on the floor. Close your eyes. Take three deep breaths at whatever pace feels natural. No counting. Just breathe.

[15-second pause for 3 breaths]

Progressive muscle relaxation works on a simple principle: you can't relax a muscle you don't know is tense. So we're going to deliberately tense each muscle group — hard, for about five seconds — then release it completely. The contrast between tension and release teaches your body what "relaxed" actually feels like. Most athletes have no idea how much residual tension they carry. You're about to find out.

We'll work from your feet up to your face. When I say "tense," squeeze that muscle group as hard as you can. When I say "release," let it go completely. Like cutting a string.

[2-second pause]

**Feet.** Curl your toes under. Squeeze them tight. Tighter. Feel the tension through the arches of your feet. Hold it... hold it...

Release. Let your feet go completely slack. Feel the difference. Feel the warmth spreading through your feet as the tension drains out.

[8-second pause]

**Calves.** Point your toes away from you, flexing your calves hard. Feel them contract. Hold... hold...

Release. Let your lower legs go heavy. Feel them sink into whatever surface is holding you.

[8-second pause]

**Quads and hamstrings.** Straighten your legs and tense your entire upper legs — front and back. Squeeze hard. These muscles work harder than almost any others in your sport. They carry a lot of tension. Hold... hold...

Release. Feel them let go. Feel the heaviness.

[8-second pause]

**Glutes.** Squeeze your glutes together as tight as you can. Hold... hold...

Release. Let them go soft. Feel your hips settle.

[8-second pause]

**Stomach.** Tighten your abs like someone's about to poke you in the gut. Brace hard. Hold... hold...

Release. Let your stomach go soft. Let it expand with your next breath. Most people hold tension here all day without knowing it.

[8-second pause]

**Hands.** Make fists. Squeeze as hard as you can. Feel the tension through your fingers, your palms, your wrists. If you grip handlebars or poles, this is where your race tension lives. Hold... hold...

Release. Open your hands. Let your fingers uncurl. Feel the blood flow back in.

[8-second pause]

**Forearms and biceps.** Bend your elbows and flex your arms like you're showing off. Squeeze hard. Hold... hold...

Release. Let your arms drop. Heavy. Limp.

[8-second pause]

**Shoulders.** This is a big one. Pull your shoulders up toward your ears. As high as you can. Feel the tension through your traps, the sides of your neck. This is where most athletes store their stress. Hold... hold...

Release. Let them drop. Far. Further than you think they should go. Feel them settle into a position that's probably lower than where they started this exercise. That's how much tension you were carrying.

[10-second pause]

**Neck.** Gently press your head back against whatever surface is behind it — the floor, the pillow, the headrest. Create tension through the back of your neck. Hold... hold...

Release. Let your head rest naturally. Feel the muscles in your neck lengthen.

[8-second pause]

**Jaw.** Clench your teeth. Squeeze your jaw shut. Athletes clench their jaws during hard efforts more than they realize — it's a chimp response, pure tension with no function. Hold... hold...

Release. Let your jaw hang open slightly. Let your tongue rest on the bottom of your mouth. Feel the difference. Your jaw should feel loose.

[8-second pause]

**Face.** Scrunch your entire face. Squeeze your eyes shut, wrinkle your nose, purse your lips. Everything tight. Hold... hold...

Release. Smooth your forehead. Let your eyes rest closed without squeezing. Let your face go completely neutral. No expression at all.

[10-second pause]

Now take a moment. Don't move yet. Scan your body from feet to face. Notice where you still feel tension — there's probably one or two spots that didn't fully let go. That's normal. Those are your tension hotspots. They're the places your body defaults to under stress. Now you know where they are.

[5-second pause]

Take three more deep breaths. Each exhale, imagine the remaining tension draining out through whatever surface you're lying or sitting on. Like water running off.

[15-second pause]

When you're ready, open your eyes. Move slowly. Don't jump up.

The goal is to recognize the difference between tension and relaxation so clearly that you can feel it in real time — on a start line, mid-race, in the middle of a hard effort. When your shoulders creep up and your jaw clenches, you'll notice it. And you'll know how to release it. Because you've practiced.

[3-second silence]

[End]
